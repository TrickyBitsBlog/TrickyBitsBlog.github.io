Why?

Nowadays when you are learning software, so much of it takes place in a carefully crafted and abstract world, far away from the hardware. Programming languages today have runtimes on top of runtimes, enabling easy portability from one architecture to another.

And away from all the fun.

Tricky Bits is all about coding against the metal, diving in deep to the world beneath the world. Where the machines matter and differences amongst the x86, ARM, Apple Silicon, and GPUs radically change what you can do and how fast you can do it.
